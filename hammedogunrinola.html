What you will learn?

Fundamentals of Software Testing: Understanding the principles, concepts, and techniques of software testing, including test planning, test case design, test execution, and defect tracking. Testing Methodologies: Exploring different testing methodologies such as black-box testing, white-box testing, functional testing, performance testing, security testing, and more. Learning when and how to apply these methodologies effectively. Test Automation: Gaining proficiency in test automation tools and frameworks like Selenium WebDriver, Appium, Cucumber, JUnit, TestNG, and others. Learning how to write automated tests to improve efficiency, accuracy, and repeatability. Programming Languages: Developing programming skills in languages commonly used for automation such as Java, Python, C#, or JavaScript. Understanding the fundamentals of object-oriented programming (OOP) and how to apply it in test automation. Test Design and Execution: Learning techniques for creating effective test cases, test scenarios, and test scripts. Understanding how to execute tests, analyze test results, and report defects in a structured manner. Continuous Integration and DevOps: Exploring the integration of testing into the software development lifecycle (SDLC) and learning how to work effectively in a DevOps environment. Understanding the role of SDETs in ensuring continuous integration, continuous testing, and continuous delivery. Test Management and Quality Assurance: Gaining knowledge of test management tools, test reporting, and metrics. Understanding the importance of quality assurance processes, standards, and best practices in software development. Collaboration and Communication: Developing strong collaboration and communication skills to work effectively with developers, product managers, and other stakeholders. Learning how to participate in agile development methodologies like Scrum or Kanban. Industry Trends and Emerging Technologies: Keeping up with the latest trends and advancements in the field of software testing and quality assurance. Exploring topics like artificial intelligence (AI) in testing, test-driven development (TDD), behavior-driven development (BDD), and more. These are just some of the key areas you can expect to learn in an SDET training program. The specific curriculum may vary depending on the training provider and the duration of the course. Remember, SDET is a dynamic field, and continuous learning and staying updated with industry practices are crucial for success.
